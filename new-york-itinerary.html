New York 5-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Central Park: Start your day by taking a relaxing stroll through Central Park. Walk through the Bethesda Terrace, visit Bow Bridge, and make your way to the Conservatory Garden.

Joe's Pizza: Enjoy a classic New York slice at Joe's Pizza in Greenwich Village.After pizza, consider grabbing a quick treat at Levain Bakery for their famous cookies.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art: Spend the afternoon exploring the vast collection at the Met. Focus on key sections, such as the Egyptian Temple of Dendur and the European Paintings.

Catch a Broadway show in the Theater District. Consider booking tickets in advance for popular shows like Hamilton, Wicked, or The Lion King.

Le Bernardin: End your day with a fine dining experience at Le Bernardin, known for its world-class seafood dishes.

Day 2

Lower East Side Tour: Start your day with a walking tour of the Lower East Side. Learn about the immigrant history of the neighborhood, and visit the Tenement Museum for a deeper look into the lives of past residents.

Kossar's Bagels & Bialys: Skip Katz's and head to Kossar's for a traditional bialy or bagel with smoked fish. This iconic bakery has been serving locals since 1936.

Chinatown & Little Italy: Wander through Chinatown and stop at some of the lesser-known spots, like Columbus Park, where you can see locals playing mahjong. Continue into Little Italy and try a cannoli at Caffe Palermo.

Rubirosa: Enjoy Italian-American cuisine at Rubirosa, a neighborhood favorite known for its thin-crust pizza and cozy atmosphere.

Day 3

The High Line: Start your day by walking the High Line, but explore the smaller streets of Chelsea afterward. Check out the local galleries in the area, like David Zwirner or Pace Gallery.

Taim Falafel: Grab a quick and delicious falafel pita at Taim in the West Village. Itâ€™s a local favorite for vegetarian street food.

Flatiron District & Madison Square Park: Instead of the Empire State Building, take a guided architecture tour of the Flatiron District to learn about its history. Relax in Madison Square Park afterward.

Rooftop Bar: Head to Mr. Purple, a rooftop bar on the Lower East Side, for relaxed vibes and great views without the heavy tourist crowds.

Day 4

Brooklyn Bridge Walk: Start early and walk across the Brooklyn Bridge, but instead of DUMBO, explore Vinegar Hill, a quiet neighborhood with cobblestone streets and charming houses.

Miss Ada: Enjoy Mediterranean-inspired small plates at Miss Ada in Fort Greene, with a cozy outdoor patio.

Bushwick Street Art Tour: Take the subway to Bushwick and do a self-guided tour of the vibrant street art. Stop by Roberta's for a quick snack or drink.

Claro: Head to Claro in Gowanus for dinner, where you can enjoy authentic Oaxacan dishes made with locally-sourced ingredients.
